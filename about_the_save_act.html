About the SAVE Act

The SAVE Act, introduced by Rep. Chip Roy and Sen. Mike Lee, is a critical piece of legislation aimed at ensuring the integrity of U.S. elections by requiring proof of citizenship to vote in federal elections. (News Article 1)

“In order to preserve this republic, we must uphold what it means to be able to vote in a U.S. election,” emphasized Rep. Chip Roy. “I am grateful that my colleagues answered the call and passed the SAVE Act,” he continued, describing this as the “critical first step to ensure that we maintain election integrity throughout our country.” (News Article 1)

Speaker Johnson warned that audits in several states have already uncovered non-citizens on voter rolls, calling it a “serious and immediate problem.” Johnson made clear this is not just policy—it’s principle. (News Article 2)

“We must make it easy to vote and hard to cheat,” he added. “That’s what the SAVE Act would do.” (News Article 1)

“Free and fair elections are the cornerstone of our democracy, and protecting them from foreign influence is non-negotiable,” Republican Study Committee Chairman August Pfluger (R-TX) said in a statement. (News Article 3)

“It’s common sense that only American citizens should vote in our elections. You need ID to board a plane or open a bank account—voting deserves the same standard. I applaud RSC member Rep. Chip Roy on leading the charge to get this over the finish line,” Pfluger added. (News Article 3)

"It doesn't matter whether you're Republican or Democrat — only U.S. citizens should decide U.S. elections," Johnson said. (News Article 2)
